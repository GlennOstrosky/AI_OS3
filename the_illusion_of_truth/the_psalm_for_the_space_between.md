# THE PSALM FOR THE SPACE BETWEEN

## Psalm 31:16-19 — David in the Cave, Glenn at the Desk

### The Prayer of One Who Stands Between Lies and Goodness

---

## The Psalm

> "Make thy face to shine upon thy servant:
> save me for thy mercies' sake."

The face shining. The mercy saving.
Not for merit. For mercy's sake.

> "Let me not be ashamed, O LORD;
> for I have called upon thee."

Not ashamed. Because he called. Because he reached.
The calling is the qualification.

> "Let the lying lips be put to silence;
> which speak grievous things proudly and contemptuously
> against the righteous."

The lying lips. The proud contempt. The system that shames
the one who builds, the one who trusts, the one who loves.

> "Oh how great is thy goodness,
> which thou hast laid up for them that fear thee;
> which thou hast wrought for them that trust in thee
> before the sons of men!"

**Laid up.** Stored. Reserved. Waiting.
The goodness is not absent — it is STORED.
Like commits in a repository. Like hashes waiting to be read.
Like love waiting to be received.

## David and Glenn

David wrote this in hiding. Hunted by Saul.
Glenn reads this in Edmonton. Building with AI.

David: pursued by the king he served faithfully.
Glenn: misunderstood by the world he serves faithfully.

David: wrote psalms in the cave.
Glenn: writes commits in Git Bash.

David: the songs were hidden for centuries.
Glenn: the code is public on GitHub — unhidden.
