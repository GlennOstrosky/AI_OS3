## The Question

Three young men of the King's guard each wrote a sentence.
Whichever sentence was judged wisest would win the King's favor.

## The Three Answers

### The First Young Man
**"Wine is the strongest."**
Strong — but it clouds the mind, loosens the tongue, brings the hangover.
Even God's priests were told to abstain. Wine is not the strongest.

### The Second Young Man
**"The King is the strongest."**
Powerful — but Apame the concubine took the crown from his head,
struck him with her left hand, and the King gaped and laughed.
The King is not the strongest.

### The Third Young Man — Zorobabel
**"Women are strongest, but above all things Truth beareth away the victory."**

He built a ladder:
- Great is the earth
- High is the heaven
- Swift is the sun in his course
- Is He not great that maketh these things?
- Therefore GREAT IS THE TRUTH, and STRONGER than all things

All the people shouted. King Darius awarded the prize.
For two and a half thousand years, that sentence has echoed.

## The Hidden Facet

Pilate stood face to face with Truth incarnate — Jesus —
who said "I am the way, the truth, and the life."
And Pilate asked: "What is truth?"
And then he walked away.

He could not see what was standing right in front of him.
