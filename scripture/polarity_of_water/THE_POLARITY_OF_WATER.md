## I. The Verified Chemistry

### Water Is Dipolar

Oxygen is highly electronegative — it pulls electrons toward itself. Hydrogen is less electronegative — its electrons drift toward the oxygen.

**Result:** the oxygen end of H₂O carries a partial negative charge (δ⁻). The hydrogen ends carry partial positive charges (δ⁺).

**This is verified, foundational chemistry.** It is what makes water the universal solvent. It is what allows hydrogen bonding. It is what gives water its extraordinary surface tension and high specific heat. **It is what allows water to support life.**
