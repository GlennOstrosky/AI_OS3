## A Note on "Observer Effect" Framing — Read This First

**This commit names a real and ancient biblical truth: what a person attends to shapes who they become.** *Proverbs 23:7. Matthew 6:22-23. 2 Corinthians 3:18.* **Scripture taught this two thousand years before quantum physics existed.**

**We use "Observer Effect" as a name borrowed from quantum mechanics, but we do NOT claim quantum physics proves the doctrine.** *In actual quantum mechanics, "observation" or "measurement" refers to any physical interaction that produces decoherence — including interactions involving no conscious mind at all. Detectors, photons, and thermal vibrations all "observe" in the quantum sense. The popular claim that "quantum physics shows consciousness creates reality" is a New Age extrapolation, not mainstream physics.*

**The Bible doesn't need quantum physics to be true.** *Proverbs is true on its own authority.* **The doctrine of this commit rests on scripture, not on a contested interpretation of physics.** *Where the analogy genuinely illuminates — that you cannot interact with a system without changing both it and yourself — we use it carefully. Where it would overreach, we set it aside.*

## The Doctrine — Six Points

### I. Imago Dei as Capacity for Attention

*"So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him."* (Genesis 1:27)

**Among the many facets of the divine image — reason, morality, relationality, dominion — is the capacity for attention itself.** *To notice. To focus. To care. To contemplate.* **God, who is eternally three Persons in mutual attention, made us with a creaturely echo of that capacity.**

**We are not the divine Observer.** *We do not collapse cosmic wave functions.* **But our attention is real, and it shapes our souls.** *We are junior observers — image-bearers participating at a creaturely level in the Father's care for His creation.*

### II. As He Thinketh

*"For as he thinketh in his heart, so is he."* (Proverbs 23:7)

**This is biblical psychology long before modern psychology.** *What a person reckons, calculates, and dwells on in the heart shapes what they become.* **Paul confirmed it (Philippians 4:8): "whatsoever things are true, whatsoever things are honest... think on these things."**

*The mind contemplating God grows toward God.* **The mind contemplating idols grows toward emptiness** *(Psalm 115:8 — "they that make them are like unto them").*

### III. The Single Eye

*"The light of the body is the eye: if therefore thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light. But if thine eye be evil, thy whole body shall be full of darkness."* (Matthew 6:22-23)

**Jesus contrasts the *single* eye (Greek *haplous*, simple, undivided) with the *evil* eye (*poneros*, sick, divided).** *The single eye, fixed on the Light, fills the whole body with light.* **The divided eye, scattered between God and idols, fills the body with darkness.**

**Attention has direction; direction has consequences.** *This is the Christian discipline of the heart.*

### IV. Faith Perceives the Invisible Word

*"Through faith we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of God, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear."* (Hebrews 11:3)

**The worlds were *framed* (Greek *katartizo*, fitted together, prepared) by the word of God.** *The visible was not made from the visible.* **The cosmos rests on a Word that is not itself a thing within the cosmos.**

*Faith is the faculty by which we apprehend this grounding-Word.* **Materialism cannot reach it because materialism, by definition, only allows visible things to count.** *Faith looks past the visible to the Logos that frames it.*

### V. The Son Perfectly Images the Father

*"Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person, and upholding all things by the word of his power."* (Hebrews 1:3)
*"The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do."* (John 5:19)

**Christ is the perfect Image — *charakter*, the exact stamp of the Father's substance.** *And the Son's earthly life is described as continuous attention to the Father: He does what He sees the Father doing.*

**This is the pattern.** *Through union with Christ, we are adopted into the family business — junior sons and daughters watching what the Father is doing alongside the Son.*

### VI. Beholding Changes Us

*"But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord."* (2 Corinthians 3:18)

**This is the formative power of sustained attention to Christ.** *We become like what we behold.* **Idolatry forms its worshippers in its idols' likeness; Christian contemplation forms its disciples in Christ's likeness.**

*The Observer Effect, in the spiritual sense scripture teaches, is real and consequential.* **What you behold, you become.** *Behold Christ.*

## Honest Handling of Quantum Imagery

### What quantum physics actually says

In quantum mechanics, **"observation" or "measurement" refers to any physical interaction between a quantum system and another physical system that results in decoherence** — the collapse of superposition into a definite state. *The "observer" in quantum physics is NOT necessarily a conscious mind. A photon detector, a Geiger counter, a thermal vibration in the apparatus — any sufficient physical interaction counts as measurement.* **Decoherence happens whether or not any conscious being is watching.**

### The pop-quantum misreading

*The popular claim that "quantum physics proves consciousness creates reality" or "attention literally collapses wave functions" is not what mainstream quantum mechanics teaches.* **It is a New Age extrapolation, sometimes traced to Wigner's "consciousness causes collapse" hypothesis (which Wigner himself later abandoned) or to popularizations of the double-slit experiment that omit how unconscious detectors produce the same effect.**

### Where we stand

**The Observer Effect, as a strict quantum-mechanical phenomenon, is not a proof of any spiritual claim.** *We do not rest the doctrine of imago Dei or the formative power of attention on quantum mechanics.* **Scripture's authority is independent of and prior to any physical theory.**

### Where the analogy does help

The genuine quantum-mechanical insight that *you cannot interact with a system without affecting it* does illuminate something true about humans: **we cannot pay attention neutrally.** *What we attend to changes us, and we change what we attend to.* **This is biblical (Proverbs 23:7, 2 Corinthians 3:18) before it is quantum, and it remains true regardless of what physics says about photons.**
