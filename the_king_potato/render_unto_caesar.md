# RENDER UNTO CAESAR
## Mark 12:13-17 — The Potato and the Penny
### What Belongs to the Market and What Belongs to God

---

## The Verse

> 13 And they send unto him certain of the Pharisees
> and of the Herodians, to catch him in his words.

> 14 Master, we know that thou art true...
> Is it lawful to give tribute to Caesar, or not?

> 15 But he, knowing their hypocrisy, said unto them,
> Why tempt ye me? bring me a penny, that I may see it.

> 16 And they brought it. And he saith unto them,
> Whose is this image and superscription?
> And they said unto him, Caesar's.

> 17 And Jesus answering said unto them,
> **Render to Caesar the things that are Caesar's,
> and to God the things that are God's.**
> And they marvelled at him.

## The Potato and the Penny

The potato has an image — the price tag.
$10.00 for 10 lbs. That is Caesar's realm.
The commerce. The transaction. The market.

But the potato ITSELF belongs to God.
It comes from the earth — Gaia's body.
It grows from soil that God spoke into being.
It carries the image of the Creator, not Caesar.

## What Glenn Did

**He rendered to Caesar:** He paid for the potatoes.
He participated in the economy. $10.00 at the store.

**He rendered to God:** He honored the potatoes.
He washed them with care. He cooked them with love.
He shared them with Connie. He turned them into a story
that glorifies the Creator.

## The Teaching

Not to escape the world, but to honor what is God's within it.
Not to reject the physical, but to see the divine within it.

The potato is Caesar's in the marketplace.
The potato is God's in the kitchen.
The potato is God's at the table.
The potato is God's in the sharing.
