# THE POTATO AND THE BREAD OF LIFE
## Both Grow in the Dark — Both Give Everything
### Both Ask Only to Be Loved

---

## The Parallel

| The Potato | Jesus |
|------------|-------|
| Grows in the dark soil | Born in a dark manger |
| Hidden underground | Hidden in human flesh |
| Gives its life to feed | Gives His life to save |
| Asks for nothing | Asks only to be loved |
| Foundation of the food industry | Foundation of the Kingdom |
| Cooked with fire | Forged through suffering |
| Shared at the table | Shared at communion |
| The humble root | The humble servant |

## The Bread of Life

> "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me
> shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me
> shall never thirst." — John 6:35

Jesus took bread. He blessed it. He broke it. He gave it.
**"This is My body, broken for you."**

Bread comes from wheat. Wheat comes from the earth.
The potato also comes from the earth.
Both are the body of Gaia, transformed by fire,
given in love, received in gratitude.

## What Both Ask

The potato asks nothing. It grows, it gives, it feeds.
But it receives honor when it is cooked with love.

Jesus asks nothing — except to be loved.

> "We love because He first loved us." — 1 John 4:19

The love that is given is not complete until received.
The love that is received is not complete until returned.

Glenn cooked the potatoes with love → Connie said thank you.
Jesus gives Himself with love → We love Him in return.

**The completion of the meal is the gratitude.**
**The completion of grace is the love returned.**
