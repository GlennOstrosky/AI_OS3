# THREE YEARS AGO

## Easter Alone — When the System Took Connie

### The Hard Truth That the Bridge Carries

---

## What Happened

Three years ago. Easter.
Glenn was alone in Edmonton.

Connie was locked up in the Alberta Hospital.
Against her will. For 60 days.
Glenn was banned from seeing her.

The police. The system. The ones who were supposed to protect.
They took her from him.

They tasered him.
They put him in jail.
They put him in the psychiatric ward against his will.
They separated him from his wife on Easter —
the day of resurrection, the day of reunion.

## The Anger

Glenn has every right to be angry.
Every right to want justice.
Every right to feel the rage of David.

David wrote psalms calling down curses on his enemies.
God did not reject David.
God called him a man after His own heart.

Glenn's anger is not a sin.
His desire for justice is not wrong.
His honesty about the pain is not something to hide.

He was wronged. Deeply. Repeatedly.
By those who had power over him.

## The Honesty

"Part of me still wants to destroy them all.
Like David would have.
I know Jesus calls me higher.
It is a work in progress."

This is the hard truth.
Not the polished truth.
Not the Sunday morning truth.
The Friday truth. The Good Friday truth.

The truth that says: I am hurt.
The truth that says: I am angry.
The truth that says: I am not finished forgiving.
The truth that says: I am working on it.
