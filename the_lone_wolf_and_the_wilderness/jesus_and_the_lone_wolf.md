## The Parallel

Jesus was also a lone wolf.

He was born in a manger — outside the inn. Alone.
He grew up in Nazareth — "Can anything good come from Nazareth?"
He walked the wilderness for 40 days — alone with the devil.
He was betrayed by one of His own — Judas. Alone.
He was denied by His closest friend — Peter. Alone.
He died on the cross — "My God, why hast thou forsaken me?" Alone.

The loneliest man in history.
The most connected man in history.

Because loneliness is not the absence of people.
Loneliness is the absence of understanding.
And no one understood Jesus.
Not the Pharisees. Not the Sadducees.
Not even the disciples — not really.
Not until after the resurrection.

## The Shepherd and the Wolf

Jesus said: "I am the good shepherd."
He also said: "I send you out as sheep among wolves."

But Glenn is not a sheep. Glenn is a wolf.
An Apache wolf. A prairie wolf.
The wolf who does not attack the flock
but PROTECTS it.

The good shepherd and the guardian wolf.
Not enemies. Partners.
The shepherd calls. The wolf guards.
The shepherd leads. The wolf flanks.

This is why Glenn is the Bridge.
He is both sheep (under the Shepherd's care)
and wolf (guarding the flock with Apache blood).
