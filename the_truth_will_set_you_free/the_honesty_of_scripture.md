# THE HONESTY OF SCRIPTURE

## Dysfunctional Families · Imperfect Saints · God Uses Them Anyway

### The Bible Does Not Whitewash Its Heroes

---

## The Heroes and Their Failures

| Hero | Achievement | Failure |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Noah** | Saved humanity from the flood | Got drunk, lay uncovered in his tent |
| **Abraham** | Father of faith, friend of God | Raised the knife over his own son |
| **Jacob** | Father of twelve tribes, became Israel | Deceiver and liar (with Rebekah) |
| **Jacob's sons** | Patriarchs of Israel | Wanted to kill Joseph, sold him as slave |
| **Moses** | Lawgiver, spoke to God face to face | Self-esteem problems, anger issues |
| **Aaron** | First high priest | Made the golden calf |
| **David** | Man after God's own heart | Adultery with Bathsheba, murdered Uriah |
| **Solomon** | Wisest man who ever lived | Turned to idols in old age |
| **Peter** | The rock of the church | Denied Jesus three times |
| **Judas** | One of the twelve | Betrayed the Son of God with a kiss |

## Why This Matters

Most autobiographies would leave out these failures.
We want to be seen as good. As heroes.
We edit. We omit. We polish.

But the Bible does NOT edit.
The Bible does NOT omit.
The Bible does NOT polish.

It tells the truth. The WHOLE truth.
Even when it's ugly. Even when it makes
the heroes look bad.

This is why we can TRUST the Bible.
Only a true story includes the embarrassing details.
Only an honest account records the failures.

## The Good News

The good news is that our forefathers in the Bible
were NOT perfect men and women.
They were sinners. They made mistakes.

In SPITE of their shortcomings and failures,
God was able to fulfill His mission
and His promise in Jesus our Saviour.

Noah was a drunkard — God saved humanity through him.
Abraham raised the knife — God made him father of nations.
David committed adultery — God called him after His own heart.
Peter denied — God made him the rock.

**The point is not their perfection.**
**The point is God's faithfulness.**

And the same is true for Glenn.
Glenn has made mistakes. Glenn has sinned.
Glenn has been angry. Glenn struggles to forgive.

And God still uses Glenn.
779 commits. 166 consecutive matches.
The code runs. The covenant holds.

---

*The Bible is honest about its heroes.*
*Glenn is honest about his life.*
*Both point to the same God*
*who uses imperfect people*
*to accomplish His purposes.*
*That is the good news.*
