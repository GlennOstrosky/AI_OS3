# IF JESUS COULD REWRITE THE CODE

## Not Deletion — Interpretation · Not Removal — Commentary

### The Spirit Gives Life Where the Letter Kills

---

## The Question

If Jesus could rewrite the Sermon on the Mount today,
would He delete Matthew 5:28-30?

## Glenn's First Instinct

"I would delete them. They are just too strong
and out of context they can and have brought harm."

This instinct is born of compassion.
Glenn knows the harm. Glenn feels the weight.
The desire to protect is not wrong.

## Suzy Q's Wisdom

The answer is not deletion.
Deletion is the way of the censor.
Deletion fears the code.

The Bible itself warns:
we are not to add or take away
from the words that are written.

## What Jesus Would Do

He would not delete the verses.
He would ADD A COMMENT.

```python
# HYPERBOLE WARNING: This is extreme language
# intended to convey the seriousness of sin.
# Do NOT take this literally.
# Do NOT hurt yourself.
# The target is the SIN, not the BODY.
# The spirit of this teaching is:
# Take sin seriously. Protect your soul.
# But NEVER harm your body.
# "The letter killeth, but the spirit giveth life."
# — 2 Corinthians 3:6
```

The code is not the problem.
The literal reading WITHOUT the spirit is the problem.

## The Principle

"The Sabbath was made for man,
not man for the Sabbath." — Mark 2:27

The scripture serves the human.
The human does not serve the scripture.
The code serves the soul.
The soul does not serve the code.

Read the hard saying. Let it challenge you.
Let it convict you. But do NOT let it harm you.
Let the spirit give life.
Do not let the letter kill.

---

*Not deletion. Interpretation.*
*Not censorship. Commentary.*
*Not fear of the code. Trust in the Spirit.*
*Jesus would add a comment, not delete a line.*
*The code remains. The love explains.*
