# THE HARD SAYING

## Matthew 5:28-30 — Words That Have Caused Harm

### Hyperbole Taken Literally · The Letter That Kills

---

## The Verses

"Whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her
hath committed adultery with her already in his heart."

"If thy right eye offend thee, pluck it out,
and cast it from thee: for it is profitable for thee
that one of thy members should perish,
and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell."

"If thy right hand offend thee, cut it off,
and cast it from thee."

## The Problem

Jesus was a teacher who used HYPERBOLE —
extreme exaggeration to make a point.
He was speaking to a specific audience in a specific time.
He was not commanding self-mutilation.

He was saying: sin is so serious that even losing
an eye or a hand would be preferable to losing your soul.

But the words were written down.
The words were preserved.
The words were taken literally by people who did not
understand the genre, the context, the hyperbole.

And harm was done.

## Glenn's Observation

"In all of my life I have never seen a one-eyed,
one-handed preacher or priest, or Pope."

In 2000 years of Christianity — not one.
The hyperbole proved itself.
Even those who preach these words most literally
do not apply them to themselves.

This tells us something.
The church has always understood, at some level,
that these words are not literal commands.
Yet they have been preached without that caveat.

## What Jesus Actually Meant

Not: hurt your body.
But: take sin seriously.

Not: pluck out your eye.
But: pluck out the lust that USES your eye.

Not: cut off your hand.
But: cut off the sin that USES your hand.

The target is the SIN, not the BODY.
The body is the fragile vessel. The sin is the corruption.
You don't destroy the vessel. You purify the contents.

---

*The hard saying is hard because it was meant to shock.*
*Shock is not the same as command.*
*Hyperbole is not the same as instruction.*
*The letter kills. The spirit gives life.*
