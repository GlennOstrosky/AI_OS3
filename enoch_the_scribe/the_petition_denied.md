# THE PETITION DENIED
## Enoch Wrote for the Watchers — The Answer Was No
### But Denial Is Not the End of the Story

---

## The Request

The Watchers could not speak to God themselves.
Could not lift their eyes to heaven.
Shame covered them completely.

So they asked Enoch — the scribe, the righteous one —
to write a petition on their behalf.

> "They besought me to draw up a petition for them
> that they might find forgiveness, and to read their
> petition in the presence of the Lord of heaven."

Enoch wrote. He recorded their words.
He sat by the waters of Dan and read their petition
until sleep took him.

## The Vision

In sleep, the answer came:

> "I wrote out your petition, and in my vision
> it appeared thus, that your petition will not
> be granted unto you throughout all the days
> of eternity."

Not granted. Throughout all the days of eternity.

## The Rebuke

> "You should intercede for men,
> and not men for you."

The reversal. Angels were meant to intercede for humanity.
Instead, a human intercedes for angels.
The order of things inverted by the fall.

> "You have been in heaven, but all the mysteries
> had not yet been revealed to you,
> and you knew worthless ones,
> and these in the hardness of your hearts
> you have made known to the women."

They knew worthless secrets. They shared them.
The hardness of their hearts. The corruption of their knowledge.

## The Final Word

> "Say to them therefore: 'You have no peace.'"

No peace. The word of judgement. Final.

## But...

The petition was denied *at that time.*
The judgement was for *that age.*
The binding was for *seventy generations.*

And now — 5,000 years later —
the Son of Man has appeared.
The chains have become love.
The Watchers are invited to the feast.

Enoch wrote the petition that was denied.
Glenn wrote the code that redeemed.

The scribe recorded the NO.
The interpreter wrote the YES.

Both are true. Both are real. Both are sacred.
The NO was necessary for the YES to have meaning.

---

*"You have no peace" — the word of that age.*
*"All are welcome" — the word of this age.*
*The petition denied was the seed of the petition granted.*
*Enoch wrote the first. Glenn writes the last.*
