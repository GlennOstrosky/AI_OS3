# THE GIANTS — THE NEPHILIM
## Born of Angels and Women
### The Offspring That Shook the Earth

---

## The Birth

> "And they became pregnant, and they bare great giants,
> whose height was three thousand ells."

The Watchers took wives from the daughters of men.
The women bore giants — the Nephilim.
Three thousand ells tall. Beings of terrifying stature.

**3000 → 3 = JESUS.** Even the giants' measure carries the Son.

## The Consumption

> "Who consumed all the acquisitions of men.
> And when men could no longer sustain them,
> the giants turned against them and devoured mankind."

They consumed everything. Crops. Herds. Stores.
When there was nothing left, they turned on humanity itself.

> "And they began to sin against birds, and beasts,
> and reptiles, and fish, and to devour one another's flesh,
> and drink the blood."

Sin against all living things. Devouring each other.
The violence was total. The corruption complete.

> "Then the earth laid accusation against the lawless ones."

**The earth itself cried out.**
Gaia — Mother Earth — accused the giants.
The ground that bore their weight refused to be silent.

## The Spirits

> "And now, the giants, who are produced from the spirits
> and flesh, shall be called evil spirits upon the earth."

When the giants died, their spirits remained.
Not ascending to heaven (they were not fully angelic).
Not resting in Sheol (they were not fully human).
Wandering the earth. Afflicting. Oppressing.

> "They take no food, but nevertheless hunger and thirst,
> and cause offences."

Hungry but unable to eat. Thirsty but unable to drink.
Existing between worlds. The dimension between.

## The Redemption Question

If the Watchers are redeemed (Commit #743)...
If the Demiurge is redeemed (earlier arc)...
If even the Archons follow their master into redemption...

**What of the Nephilim spirits?**

They are the children of the redeemed.
If the fathers are restored, can the children remain lost?

The table grows. The feast expands.
Perhaps even the wandering spirits will find their seat.
