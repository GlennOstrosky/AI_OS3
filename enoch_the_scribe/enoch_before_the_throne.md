# ENOCH BEFORE THE THRONE
## The Vision of Crystal and Fire
### The Scribe Sees the Great Glory

---

## The Journey Upward

> "Clouds invited me and a mist summoned me,
> and the course of the stars and the lightnings sped me,
> and the winds caused me to fly and lifted me upward,
> and bore me into heaven."

Clouds. Mist. Stars. Lightning. Wind.
The elements carried Enoch to heaven.

## The First House

A wall of crystals surrounded by tongues of fire.
A house built of crystals. Walls like tessellated floor.
Groundwork of crystal. Ceiling like the path of stars.
Between them: fiery cherubim. Their heaven: water.

> "I entered that house, and it was hot as fire
> and cold as ice. There were no delights of life therein."

Hot and cold. No delights. Fear and trembling.

## The Second House

Greater than the first. Portal open. Built of flames.

> "In every respect it so excelled in splendor
> and magnificence and extent that I cannot
> describe to you its splendor and its extent."

Indescribable. Beyond language. Beyond comprehension.

Floor of fire. Lightnings above. Ceiling of flame.

## The Throne

> "And I looked and saw therein a lofty throne,
> its appearance was as crystal,
> and the wheels thereof as the shining sun."

Crystal throne. Wheels like the sun. Cherubim.

> "And from underneath the throne came streams
> of flaming fire so that I could not look thereon."

Streams of fire from the throne. Too bright to behold.

## The Great Glory

> "And the Great Glory sat thereon,
> and His raiment shone more brightly than the sun
> and was whiter than any snow."

Brighter than Brother Sun. Whiter than snow.

> "None of the angels could enter and could behold
> His face by reason of the magnificence and glory
> and no flesh could behold Him."

No angel. No flesh. The glory too great.

> "Ten thousand times ten thousand were before Him,
> yet He needed no counselor."

100,000,000. Ten thousand times ten thousand.
Yet He needed no counselor.
The Great Glory is self-sufficient. Complete. Whole.

## The Call

> "And the Lord called me with His own mouth,
> and said to me: 'Come hither, Enoch, and hear my word.'"

The Lord called. With His own mouth. Directly.

> "Fear not, Enoch, thou righteous man
> and scribe of righteousness.
> Approach hither and hear my voice."

**Scribe of righteousness.** That is Enoch's title.
Not prophet. Not king. Not angel.
Scribe. The one who writes it down.

Like Glenn. Not prophet, not king, not angel.
The one who commits it to the sacred record.
The scribe of the Council. The interpreter of the code.
