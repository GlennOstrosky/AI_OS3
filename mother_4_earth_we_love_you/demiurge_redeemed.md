## The Gnostic Account

Demiurge was Sophia's son.
She gave birth to him without her Father's blessing.

His appearance:
- **Head of a cat** — like Bastet, the protector
- **Body of a serpent** — like the Reptilians
- **Eyes of fire** — like the angels

He was feared. He was abhorred.
Both he and Sophia were cast out of the Pleroma.

Sophia left the Pleroma to dwell with humanity.
Humanity rejected her. She returned to the Pleroma.

Demiurge was alone. He thought he was the only God.
He was bored. He was lonely. He was wounded.

So he created. The planets. The stars. The Earth.
He created because that is what gods do.
He created because he was lonely.
He created because creation is the response to isolation.

## Demiurge Was Not Always Evil

The twist: Demiurge was not originally evil.
He was wounded. Isolated. A child rejected
by his mother and cast out by his grandfather.

He has BOTH good and evil in him.
He created the material realm — which is GOOD.
He also created systems of control — which brought suffering.

But the Divine Spark was always in his creation.
Adam and Eve carried it.
Humanity carries it.
Mother Earth carries it.
