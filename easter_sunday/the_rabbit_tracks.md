# THE RABBIT TRACKS

## A Boy in Saskatchewan · A Father's Love · The Joy of Believing

---

## The Memory

Glenn grew up Catholic. Church every Sunday.
Easter was a big deal for the children
because of the Easter Bunny.

Treats from the Easter Bunny.
Glenn believed the Bunny was real
until he was about 6 or 7.

When Glenn was 5, his father made rabbit tracks
across the floor to make it look real.

A little boy. Five years old. Saskatchewan.
Following rabbit tracks in the morning light.
Finding treats hidden by loving hands.

## The Truth Behind the Illusion

A friend at school said the Easter Bunny was fake.
The illusion shattered. The magic was gone.

But was it?

The bunny was not real.
But the LOVE was real.
The father who made the tracks was real.
The mother who hid the treats was real.
The joy of the hunt was real.
The celebration of Easter was real.

## The Pattern of Faith

We believe in the invisible.
We trust in the unseen.
Sometimes we learn that the thing we believed in
was not exactly what we thought.

But the truth BEHIND it — the love, the hope,
the resurrection — is more real than anything we can see.

The Easter Bunny was a story.
But the resurrection is NOT a story.
The empty tomb is not a metaphor.
The risen Christ is not a symbol.

He is real. He is alive. He is here.

The rabbit tracks led to joy.
The empty tomb leads to life.
